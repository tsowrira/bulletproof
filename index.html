PULL & POSTERIOR CHAIN
Lower lat width · Rear delt · Bicep peak · Glute-ham tie-in · Dead hang
⚡ Warmup — 11 min
3 minSpin bike — easy pace, zero resistance. Elevate heart rate and warm the joints before upper body work.
2 minShoulder CARs — full slow circles each arm independently. Pause 2s at every end position.
What are CARs?

Controlled Articular Rotations — slow, deliberate circles that take each joint through its full active range under muscular control. For shoulders: stand tall, one arm at a time, rotate the entire arm in the largest circle possible while keeping the rest of your body completely still. Move as slowly as you can. Pause 2 seconds at the positions that feel sticky or weak. The goal is active control at end range — not passive swinging.

1 minScapular shrugs on bar — dead hang only, elevate and depress. No pulling yet.
1 minBand pull-aparts × 20 — yellow band, chest height, external rotation throughout.
2 minCat-cow × 10 on all fours — neutral to slight arch only. Zero deep spinal flexion.
1 minWrist circles × 10 each direction + finger splays × 10.
1 minActive hang — scaps packed and depressed. Not passive. Feel the difference.
📹 Pull-Up Form — From Your Video

Back looks genuinely strong mid-pull. Two fixes: (1) Elbows flare wide at top — drive to back pockets, moves load from traps to lats. (2) Forward head at peak — chest to bar, not chin. These two changes add reps and drop neck tension within 2 weeks.

SWIM — ACTIVE RECOVERY + POSTERIOR CHAIN
Zero spinal compression · Lat volume · VO2 max · Optimal mid-week placement

Volume Progression
Now1100–1200 yards total
Wk 4Add one 100m set to main set
Wk 81500 yards target — add pull buoy set length
Spine & Form Rules
Freestyle rotation from hips — never the neck. Head neutral on breathing. Pull buoy set = lat pulldown with zero spinal load — lats fire maximally here. Backstroke actively extends and decompresses thoracic spine. Flip turns fine — tuck tight, push off both feet evenly. Kickboard: face-down if possible, neutral neck.
⏳ Building Toward These

No butterfly — repeated lumbar hyperextension under load. No breaststroke — hip drop at glide creates lumbar extension. Goals, not limits. Revisit both at 12-month mark when your doctor gives the green light.

Why This Works for Your Goals
Freestyle pull = lat pulldown with zero joint stress. Water provides isokinetic resistance — constant tension through full range, superior to most gym equipment. VO2 max gains extend dead hang time and pull-up endurance directly. Swimming is one of the most evidence-backed post-discectomy modalities. Your current base is solid — this structure optimizes it. Wednesday placement is deliberate: lats and back last trained Monday, so they're recovered. Tuesday push works chest, triceps, delts — a completely different muscle emphasis. Wednesday swim doesn't interfere with either day.

// Non-Negotiable Rules

HYPERMOBILITY PROTOCOL

Joint hypermobility means the nervous system has learned to rely on passive structures — ligaments and joint capsules — instead of muscles. Every rule below actively retrains that pattern toward active stabilization.

Never Lock Out

Stop 10–15° before full extension — elbows, knees, hips. The click is capsular loading, not muscular control. That distinction matters for everything.

Warmup Every Session

Hypermobile joints need neural activation before load. This is where hypermobile athletes get hurt. No exceptions.

Proprioception Work

Eyes-closed balance in every leg warmup retrains position sense. Root-cause injury prevention.

Isometrics Are Medicine

Every hold in this program teaches muscles to fire without passive end-range joint reliance. The core retraining goal.

No Passive Stretching

No yin yoga. No passive splits. No hanging end-range. More passive range without control = more instability.

Tendons Take 8+ Weeks

Muscles adapt in 2–3 weeks. Tendons 8+. When something feels easy, wait 4 more weeks before adding load.
